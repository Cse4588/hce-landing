Common questions

Things people ask before buying.

Is this for employees or HR professionals?

Both, but it's written for the person at home dealing with a bill — not for the HR team that selected the plan. If you're an employee trying to understand your own insurance, this is for you. If you're an HR Director who wants something to hand your workforce, it works for that too. There's a note at the bottom of this page about bulk access.

Will this work with my specific insurance plan?

Yes. Healthcare Essentials is not plan-specific — it explains how health insurance works as a system: what deductibles, out-of-pocket maximums, and in-network vs. out-of-network mean in practice, how to read any EOB, and how the appeal process works regardless of your insurer. The concepts apply whether you're on a Blue Cross PPO, an Aetna HMO, a UnitedHealthcare HDHP, or anything else.

My employer gave me a benefits guide during open enrollment. How is this different?

Your employer's guide tells you what you have. Healthcare Essentials tells you how to use it. Employer guides describe your specific plan's coverage limits and copays. They don't explain how to appeal a denied claim, how to read the EOB that arrives six weeks later, or how to figure out whether a bill is accurate. That's the gap this fills.

What format is it and how do I get it?

Healthcare Essentials is delivered as a downloadable PDF immediately after purchase via Payhip. You'll receive a download link by email. It's readable on any device — phone, tablet, or computer — and fully printable if you prefer paper. It's yours to keep, share with family members, and come back to whenever you need it.

Does the Medicare guide cover Medicare Advantage plans?

Yes. The Medicare section covers traditional Medicare (Parts A and B), Medicare Advantage (Part C), Part D prescription coverage, and how Medicare coordinates with an employer plan for employees who are still working at 65. It also covers the most common mistakes people make during the Medicare enrollment window and how to avoid them.
